# Inverse Reinforcement Learning
Inverse reinforcement learning is a subfield of agentic AI that involves learning an agent's goals or rewards by observing its behavior. This concept is essential in situations where the reward function is unknown or difficult to specify. Inverse reinforcement learning can be used to learn from demonstrations, such as learning to drive a car by observing a human driver.

This technique has various practical applications, including robotics, autonomous vehicles, and healthcare. For instance, in robotics, inverse reinforcement learning can be used to learn a robot's goals and preferences by observing its behavior, allowing it to adapt to new situations.
